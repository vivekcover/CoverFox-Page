5 vital features which make
comparing health plans easier.

Hospital Network in your area

Health Insurance policies generally provide cashless treatment facility in their network of hospitals. You need to compare how many hospitals are covered in your area for cashless, for your shortlisted Insurance companies.

Room eligibility

Room Rent Limit or Room Category capping, if ignored, could turn out to be the biggest problem in your health insurance policy. In case any of your family members are hospitalized, if you opt for a higher room than your eligibility, your entire hospitalization bill could be proportionately deducted, and hence lead to a massive loss for you.

Copay feature

Copay is a percentage of the admissible claim that you will be required to pay. Insurance companies would pay the rest of the admissible claim. Check for the Co-pay clause in the policy which could sneak into the plan, when you are getting older.

Annual Premium

While it is not advisable to compare costs alone when choosing a plan, it is practical to compare shortlisted plans offering similar features per your requirement. Ideally, there should not be a lot of variation in the premium of similar features. Be prepared to pay a higher premium, in case you want the plan to cover each and every small health cost such as OPD bills, dental and opthalmic costs etc.

Other important indicators of a good
health plan and insurance company would be:

Waiting periods & Exclusions:

All policies have a stipulated time period before they start covering certain kind of diseases. There are also some illnesses that may fall out of the range of coverage that a medical insurance plan offers. This should be kept in mind

Claim history / Word of Mouth:

If a company does not have satisfactory reviews around claim settlement or there is substantial delay in turn around times, it would be wise to not choose policies from such companies. A good broker, thus helps in their recommendation of plans from companies with good service levels.

Restore feature:

Few policies now come with the restore benefit which helps you to restore your coverage amount even after you claim it once. This assists you to share the burden of unforeseen medical costs that may strike more than once a year

Bonus policy:

Most policies in the market today have attractive bonus incentives for those who don’t claim from the policy during the year. Some policies offer you an increase in your coverage amount in the subsequent year while others give a discount on your renewal premium.
